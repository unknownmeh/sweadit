A Poem

The Weight of
Silence

He was avoided, yet he said no word, His silence louder than screams unheard. Unseen, unvalued, his heart grew tight, His pain hidden out of sight.

Ignored by many, betrayed by trust, His fragile soul turned into dust. He lost himself in a world so cold, Where stories of pain remain untold.

He cared too much, his heart exposed, Only to find it cruelly opposed. Respect he gave, insults he received, A life of giving, yet always deceived.

Willing to break for other's gain, Yet left with scars that echo the pain. If he had chosen himself to defend, Perhaps his heart wouldn't break or bend.

The pain of an introvert is quiet and deep, Their battles fought where no one can peep. In their silence, a storm resides, A fragile heart that the world divides.
