Act I — The Boy

A Soul Shaped by Stillness

Lucian is a boy who wanted to live peacefully and happily. At a small age he felt many feelings and experienced the worst scenarios. The time was very difficult — we can't imagine how it feels to navigate paths without any clues.

Lucian learned people by reading their minds — captured how they spoke to him when they had a need, and how they treated him when they were done. By being silent, he learned many things. He was very capable at some things and painfully aware of others. Although he was scared all the time, that fear became his reason for silence. Everybody is talkative — but he is different.

"His silence speaks more compared to a hundred talkative people."

He imagined a world written in books — a beautiful vision filled with solitude and happiness. But the world in books is only one face. Deep down, everyone is infected by a kind of virus which kills their true personality. The reality is totally different, and he was unable to accept sometimes why the world was so cruel to him.

👁️

Betrayal
Act II — The First Scar

The Sting of Being Unseen

Lucian sat in the study hall, focused on his notes, trying to absorb as much as he could for the upcoming exam. The soft hum of murmured discussions and the occasional shuffle of pages filled the room — a comforting rhythm that helped him concentrate.

Just as he was beginning to settle into his work, a call came from the warden, summoning him to the office. Confused, Lucian hesitated. A sense of unease crept into his mind, but he shook it off, assuming it was something minor.

The Moment

As he stepped into the office, the atmosphere felt heavy and tense. Before he could ask why he had been called — the warden's hand struck his face. His books slipped from his hand and hit the floor with a thud. Tears threatened to spill as he looked up in disbelief, his voice trembling: "What did I do?"

"The sting of the slap wasn't just physical — it pierced his heart. He felt betrayed by the very adults who were supposed to guide and protect him."

Summoning his courage, he spoke with trembling but firm words: "I have no idea about this letter. Please, ask others or check anything to confirm — it's not mine." The truth was eventually uncovered. Another student admitted to writing the letter, and Lucian was absolved.

But when Lucian reported the true culprit to the warden — there were no consequences. No reprimand. No apology. The injustice had been dismissed as nothing more than a small misunderstanding. Lucian stood there, feeling the sting of disappointment and disbelief.

"He realized that being a good person was far more difficult than being bad. It only took one wrong action to tarnish a person's entire character. Most importantly, he came to understand the deep pain behind being punished for something you didn't do."

Alone
Act III — The Quiet Truth

The Weight of Silence

He was someone who was ignored several times by all his classmates and relations. That lone wolf stood out without fear among the group of wolves — beside them with unimaginable psychological power, yet never given chances, time, love, or support.

The hard truth about introverts in this world — they are used as a backup for support, and not as a priority. No one wants to make them a priority just because of their silence. But no one can know the peace behind the silence of an introvert.

"Hurting someone who understands you is easy because you believe they will know you without saying a word. They may comprehend everyone else's feelings, but no one can truly grasp their emotions when they are hurt by their loved ones."

🌊

🦢

Like Swans

To gain an introvert's friendship, you must earn their trust as you would their loyalty. Their circle is small — fewer than five — but runs infinitely deep.

🪞

Like a Mirror

By observing every action, sound, and moment, he became a mirror — reflecting back whatever the world threw at him.

Introverts are keenly aware when others are using them, often sensing manipulation through observations and empathy. Over time, the accumulation of hurt turns into emotional distance. Their silence isn't indifference — it is a shield against further emotional pain.

We, as humans, are not all the same. Introverts tend to lose themselves in helping others, often struggling to say "no" and overcommitting at the cost of their own well-being. Their expectations are simple — a little talk, honest smiles, and only transparency.

Storm
Act IV — The Storm

Silent Storm

The storm had been
brewing for years

If introverts were to seek revenge, the world would feel the weight of it like never before. This silence is not the absence of emotion — it is the accumulation of hurt, betrayal, and frustration suppressed over time. They may not react immediately, but when pushed to their breaking point, the intensity of their response would be overwhelming.

"An introvert's revenge would not be impulsive or loud. It would be calculated and deliberate — a reflection of their deep understanding of the people and situations that have wronged them."

The true danger lies not in the moment of revenge itself, but in the silence that precedes it. When they finally decide to strike back, it's as if the world is hit by a storm that had been brewing for years. In that moment, no one would feel safe.

The voice of an ordinary man may frighten a hundred, but the silence of an introvert can shatter the world or destroy millions of deceitful souls. Every introvert is a predictor of what's to come, sensing the truth without needing to ask a single question.
